Tests conducted in the laboratory with real samples of Kevlar, Nylon and Cotton

EXP_01 THREAD TEST: KEVLAR vs NYLON ELASTICITY
// EXPERIMENT DESCRIPTION
Two threads are suspended and loaded with identical weights. The difference in behavior reveals the fundamental mechanical properties of each material.

Left — Kevlar Thread: The thread barely moves and does not sag. Kevlar exhibits excellent dimensional stability and extreme rigidity — it shows virtually no elongation under tension.

Right — Nylon Thread: The thread stretches and sags significantly downward. Nylon is a highly elastic material that elongates considerably under the same static load.

The applied weights are identical — the difference in deformation comes exclusively from the molecular properties of the two materials.

EXP_02 CUTTING TEST CUTTING
// EXPERIMENT DESCRIPTION
We attempt to cut Kevlar with a standard box cutter with a new blade. The applied force is significant, but the fiber resists direct cutting.

The woven structure of the fiber distributes the force along multiple threads, preventing the cut from propagating. This is why Kevlar is used in cut-resistant gloves and armor.

EXP_03 KEVLAR BURNING THERMAL
// EXPERIMENT DESCRIPTION
Kevlar is directly exposed to flame. We observe that the fiber does not melt and does not ignite — it carbonizes locally, maintaining the structural integrity of unaffected areas.

The decomposition point of Kevlar is ~450°C, compared to polyethylene (~120°C). This property makes it essential in firefighter equipment and thermal armor.

DECOMP. TEMP. ~450°C

EXP_04 NYLON BURNING THERMAL
// EXPERIMENT DESCRIPTION — COMPARISON
Nylon (aliphatic polyamide) is exposed to the same flame. Unlike Kevlar, nylon melts and contracts rapidly, forming burning droplets.

The melting point of nylon is ~220°C — half the decomposition temperature of Kevlar. The behavior is completely different, confirming the superiority of the aromatic structure in Kevlar.
MATERIAL NYLON PA6
MELTING TEMP. ~220°C
BEHAVIOR MELTS ✗
EXP_05 COTTON BURNING THERMAL
// EXPERIMENT DESCRIPTION — COMPARISON
Cotton burns continuously and rapidly, without self-extinguishing, producing visible flame and ash. The cellulose in cotton is highly flammable.

Comparing the three materials, Kevlar demonstrates clear thermal superiority: controlled carbonization vs. melting (Nylon) vs. complete burning (Cotton).
